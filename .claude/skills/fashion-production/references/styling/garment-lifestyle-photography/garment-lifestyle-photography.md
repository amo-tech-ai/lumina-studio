# Garment Lifestyle Photography — Location Shoot Briefs

## When to Use This Skill

Use when you need to:
- Plan a real lifestyle shoot on location (not studio)
- Create location scouting checklists
- Define scene setups with props, talent, and environment
- Write weather contingency plans
- Build the lifestyle section of a production package
- Direct authentic, unforced lifestyle moments on set

## Core Principle

**Lifestyle photography shows products in real life. That means real locations, real weather, real logistics.** This skill produces location briefs, scouting checklists, and on-set direction — NOT AI image prompts.

## Lifestyle Contexts (5 Types)

### Context 1: Urban

**Audience:** 20-35, city dwellers, fashion-forward
**Mood:** Energetic, modern, confident

```
URBAN LIFESTYLE BRIEF:

Location Types:
  □ Café with large windows (natural light)
  □ Clean urban street (architectural background)
  □ Rooftop terrace (skyline backdrop)
  □ Modern interior (co-working, gallery, hotel lobby)
  □ Public transit station (editorial edge)

Scouting Checklist:
  □ Light direction at shoot time (which windows face what?)
  □ Foot traffic level (busy = distracting, quiet = better control)
  □ Background control (can we restrict the area?)
  □ Power access (if supplemental lighting needed)
  □ Permit requirements (public vs private property)
  □ Noise level (affects video if shooting reels)
  □ Parking for equipment load-in

Props:
  □ Coffee cup (branded removed or neutral)
  □ Tote bag or leather bag (brand-appropriate)
  □ Phone (in hand, not screen visible)
  □ Sunglasses (if eyewear is product category)
  □ Newspaper/magazine (editorial prop)

Talent Direction:
  "Walking with purpose — you're heading somewhere you want to be."
  "Pause at the window — something caught your eye."
  "Sitting with coffee — you're early, enjoying the quiet."

Camera:
  Lens: 35mm (environmental context)
  Aperture: f/2.8-f/4 (subject pop, background context)
  Approach: Documentary style, capture between direction
```

### Context 2: Casual

**Audience:** 25-40, relaxed, quality-focused
**Mood:** Comfortable, warm, authentic

```
CASUAL LIFESTYLE BRIEF:

Location Types:
  □ Home interior (living room, kitchen, bedroom)
  □ Neighborhood café (cozy, not trendy)
  □ Local park or garden (greenery, benches)
  □ Bookshop or market (authentic local)
  □ Porch/balcony (relaxed outdoor)

Scouting Checklist:
  □ Natural light quality (north-facing windows best)
  □ Interior decoration matches brand DNA (not competing style)
  □ Clutter control (real but not messy)
  □ Color palette of environment (walls, furniture, textiles)
  □ Access for equipment (lights, reflectors if needed)

Props:
  □ Coffee/tea mug (ceramic, not paper)
  □ Book or magazine (spine visible, not screen)
  □ Throw blanket (texture, complements brand palette)
  □ Plant or flowers (natural, not plastic)
  □ Pet (if brand-appropriate — discuss with client)

Talent Direction:
  "Morning routine — you just made coffee, sitting by the window."
  "Reading on the couch — comfortable, not performing."
  "Walking the dog — genuine interaction, laughing."

Camera:
  Lens: 50mm (natural perspective)
  Aperture: f/2.8 (warm, intimate depth)
  Approach: Unobtrusive, observe-and-capture
```

### Context 3: Active

**Audience:** 22-40, fitness/outdoor enthusiasts
**Mood:** Dynamic, free, adventurous

```
ACTIVE LIFESTYLE BRIEF:

Location Types:
  □ Hiking trail (scenic, manageable terrain)
  □ Beach/waterfront (sunrise or sunset timing)
  □ Outdoor running path (clean, scenic route)
  □ Rock/climbing (dramatic backdrop)
  □ Yoga/fitness space (natural light studios)

Scouting Checklist:
  □ Terrain difficulty (crew can carry equipment?)
  □ Sun position at shoot time (golden hour timing)
  □ Wind conditions (affects hair, garment movement)
  □ Water/weather exposure risk (protect equipment)
  □ Cell signal (for communication, tethering)
  □ Distance from vehicle (equipment load-in logistics)
  □ Restroom access for model changes

Props:
  □ Water bottle (neutral or brand-aligned)
  □ Backpack (if relevant to product)
  □ Yoga mat, resistance band (activity-specific)
  □ Bicycle, surfboard (if brand context)
  □ Nothing (many active shots need no props)

Talent Direction:
  "Mid-run — I'll capture you on the path, just go."
  "Summit pause — look out at the view, catch your breath."
  "Stretching before activity — natural, not posed."

Camera:
  Lens: 35mm or 24-70mm zoom (environmental, flexible)
  Aperture: f/4 (balance subject and landscape)
  Shutter: 1/500s+ (freeze movement)
  Approach: Action/sport photography style, capture real movement
```

### Context 4: Professional

**Audience:** 28-50, career-focused, polished
**Mood:** Confident, purposeful, refined

```
PROFESSIONAL LIFESTYLE BRIEF:

Location Types:
  □ Modern office (clean, minimal — not cluttered desk)
  □ Co-working space (open, bright, architectural)
  □ Hotel lobby (luxury, business traveler)
  □ Conference setting (thought leadership)
  □ City sidewalk during commute (purposeful walk)

Scouting Checklist:
  □ Background appropriate for professional context
  □ Branding visible in environment? (remove competing logos)
  □ Lighting quality (office fluorescents = bad, add supplemental)
  □ Noise/activity (real office has people — plan around schedules)
  □ IT approval if shooting in corporate space

Props:
  □ Laptop (open, screen not visible to camera)
  □ Leather portfolio or notebook
  □ Coffee (takeaway cup, neutral branding)
  □ Phone (in hand, businesslike)
  □ Briefcase or professional bag (if relevant product)

Talent Direction:
  "Walking into the meeting — you're prepared and confident."
  "Working at desk — focused, not posing for camera."
  "Handshake or greeting — professional warmth."

Camera:
  Lens: 50mm or 85mm (professional, flattering)
  Aperture: f/2.8-f/4 (clean background, subject focus)
  Approach: Corporate editorial — polished but authentic
```

### Context 5: Social

**Audience:** 22-38, social, event-oriented
**Mood:** Elevated, celebratory, warm

```
SOCIAL LIFESTYLE BRIEF:

Location Types:
  □ Restaurant (upscale, good ambient lighting)
  □ Bar/lounge (moody, atmospheric)
  □ Gallery opening (cultural, sophisticated)
  □ Rooftop event (sunset, city views)
  □ Dinner party setting (intimate, styled table)

Scouting Checklist:
  □ Ambient light level (often dark — plan supplemental)
  □ Table/seating arrangements (can we control layout?)
  □ Background aesthetics (other diners visible? Controllable?)
  □ Reservation requirements (private area if possible)
  □ Permission to photograph (some venues restrict)

Props:
  □ Wine glass or cocktail (elegant, brand-appropriate)
  □ Clutch or evening bag (if product)
  □ Menu (leather-bound, elegant)
  □ Candle/flowers on table (ambient props)
  □ Dessert plate (styled, photogenic)

Talent Direction:
  "Arriving at the restaurant — walking in, coat off the shoulder."
  "Mid-conversation — laughing naturally, glass in hand."
  "Leaving — standing, putting on jacket, looking back."

Camera:
  Lens: 35mm or 50mm (intimate social setting)
  Aperture: f/1.8-f/2.8 (low light, atmospheric blur)
  ISO: Higher acceptable (up to 3200 — authentic low-light feel)
  Approach: Documentary/reportage style — capture real moments
```

## Weather Contingency Plans

```
WEATHER CONTINGENCY — Location Shoots

SUNNY (harsh midday):
  Action: Add 4x4 diffusion panel to soften direct sun
  Backup: Move to shaded area (building shadow, tree cover)
  Timing: Shift schedule to avoid 11am-2pm direct overhead sun

OVERCAST (ideal):
  Action: Shoot as planned — this is the best natural light
  Note:   Colors appear more saturated, shadows are soft
  Adjust: May need reflector for catchlights in eyes

RAIN:
  Action: Move to covered outdoor area (awning, porch, archway)
  Backup: Switch to indoor location from backup list
  Note:   Wet surfaces = great reflections (shoot if rain stops)
  Protect: Cover all equipment, keep camera bags under cover

WIND:
  Action: Use wind to advantage for fabric movement shots
  Limit:  If >20mph, hair control becomes impossible
  Backup: Move to sheltered location or windbreak
  Protect: Secure light stands, reflectors (sandbags required)

GOLDEN HOUR SHIFT:
  Action: Check sunset time day-before, confirm schedule
  Note:   Golden hour moves ~2 min/day — verify exact timing
  Buffer: Arrive 30 min before golden hour (setup + test shots)
```
